Synopsys said it would cut about 10% of its workforce, roughly 2,000 roles, as it restructured after the Ansys deal and redirected resources toward higher-growth areas including AI-driven design and simulation.

Synopsys announced on November 12 that it will reduce its workforce by about 10%, or roughly 2,000 employees... The company said the cuts will allow it to redirect investment toward growth opportunities, particularly in artificial intelligence and advanced node technologies.

This is weaker than the cleaner Reuters roundup entries because merger synergies are also central, but the cited resource shift toward AI/growth areas is explicit enough to keep as a border-line verified addition.
